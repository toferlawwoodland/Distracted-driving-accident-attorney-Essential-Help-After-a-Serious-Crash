## Distracted driving accident attorney: What Victims Should Know After a Collision

Distracted driving occurs when a driver’s attention is removed from the responsibility of safely operating a vehicle.

Safe driving requires:

- Watching traffic conditions
- Maintaining vehicle control
- Following road signs
- Reacting quickly to hazards

When a driver becomes distracted, their ability to respond to dangerous situations may be reduced.

Common distractions include:

- Texting or using a cellphone
- Adjusting navigation systems
- Eating or drinking
- Talking with passengers
- Looking away from the road
- Using electronic devices

These actions may seem harmless, but they can lead to severe collisions.

## Why Distracted Driving Accidents Are Complicated

A distracted driving claim requires more than proving that an accident occurred. Victims must often show that another driver’s lack of attention caused the collision and resulted in damages.

Important factors may include:

- Driver behavior before the crash
- Phone usage
- Traffic violations
- Accident circumstances
- Injury severity
- Financial losses

Unlike some accidents where fault is immediately clear, distracted driving may require detailed investigation.

Evidence is especially important because a driver may deny using a phone or engaging in another distracting activity.

## Types of Distracted Driving

### Visual Distraction

Visual distraction happens when drivers remove their eyes from the road.

Examples include:

- Looking at text messages
- Checking navigation apps
- Looking at passengers
- Searching for objects inside the vehicle

### Manual Distraction

Manual distraction occurs when drivers remove their hands from the steering wheel.

Examples include:

- Holding a phone
- Adjusting vehicle controls
- Reaching for items

### Cognitive Distraction

Cognitive distraction happens when a driver’s mind is not focused on driving.

Examples include:

- Stress
- Emotional conversations
- Fatigue
- Daydreaming

A driver may appear alert but still fail to recognize dangerous situations.

## Common Accidents Caused by Distracted Drivers

### Rear-End Collisions

Distracted drivers often fail to notice slowing traffic, resulting in rear-end crashes.

### Intersection Accidents

Drivers who are distracted may:

- Run red lights
- Fail to yield
- Ignore crossing vehicles

### Pedestrian Accidents

Drivers who are not paying attention may fail to notice pedestrians.

### Highway Accidents

At high speeds, a brief distraction can cause devastating crashes.
